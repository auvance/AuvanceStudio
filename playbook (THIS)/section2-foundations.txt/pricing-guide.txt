<aside>

Pricing your services can feel overwhelming, but it’s one of the most critical elements of running a successful creative business. A well-thought-out pricing strategy can elevate your perceived value, close more deals, and ensure you’re building a sustainable business.

</aside>

---

<aside>

### **If You're Just Getting Started**

</aside>

If you're completely new to the industry and don’t have a portfolio yet, it’s okay to work for free initially to build credibility and showcase your skills. Here’s how to approach it:

- **Focus on your niche:** Choose industries or clients you’d eventually like to work with and offer them complimentary work in exchange for portfolio pieces and testimonials.
- **Target high-value clients:** Even if you're working for free, aim for businesses or brands that will look impressive in your portfolio.
- **Keep it professional:** Even free work deserves clear expectations and deliverables.
- **Leverage the results:** Once you have 3-5 strong projects in your portfolio, start charging confidently.

---

<aside>

### **Aligning Pricing with Your Offer**

</aside>

Your pricing should reflect the value you’re providing, not just the time or effort you’re putting in. Clients don’t care how hard you worked—they care about results. Here’s how to approach it:

- **Focus on outcomes:** If your work can help a client generate more revenue, attract more customers, or improve their brand, price your services based on that potential value.
- **Understand the client's budget:** High-end clients expect high-end pricing. Charging too low can make them question your quality.
- **Price for expertise, not hours:** Clients pay for your experience, creativity, and ability to deliver results—not just the time you spent executing the work.

---

<aside>

### **Pricing Varies by Industry, Niche, and Location**

</aside>

When setting your prices, remember that what works in one industry or location may not work in another. Here's what to keep in mind:

- **Industry/Niche Expectations:** Different industries have varying budgets and expectations. For example, luxury brands will likely pay more than small local businesses, but they’ll also expect higher production value and deliverables.
- **Geographic Factors:** Pricing also depends heavily on your location. Clients in high-cost-of-living areas or metropolitan cities generally have higher budgets than rural or smaller areas. Research the standard rates in your region to avoid underpricing or overpricing.
- **Adaptability is Key:** You may need to adjust your pricing based on the client’s industry or the specific market conditions in your area.

💡 *Pro Tip: Start by researching competitors or similar businesses in your industry and region to get an idea of standard pricing benchmarks.*

---

<aside>

### **Determining Your Base Rate**

</aside>

Your pricing structure will depend on your type of work. For service-based creatives (video, photography, production), a day rate is common. For others (designers, strategists, social media managers), this might translate into project-based or hourly pricing.

- **Half-Day Rate (4–6 hours of work):** Around **€800**
- **Full-Day Rate (8–10 hours of work):** Around **€1,200**

💡Important Note for Videographers / Photographers:

Your day rate only covers your time on-site for shooting. It does not include editing, post production, or any additional fees tied to your packages, such as pre-production planning, equipment rentals, or travel expenses. These should be factored into your package pricing separately.

The same goes for other creative businesses with multiple levels of production process. 

---

<aside>

### **Package Creation**

</aside>

Packages make pricing simpler for clients and allow you to upsell effectively. 

I would recommend having at least 2 options. Your base (essential) package and a more complete/premium option. 

You don’t want to surpass 3 options, that is the sweetspot. Having multiple options might confuse your client and create decision fatigue. 

The structure below is based on a video/content example, but the same logic applies to any creative service.

Here’s a basic breakdown of how to structure them:

**Basic Package**

- Entry-level offer for smaller budgets.
- Limited deliverables: e.g. 4 hour shoot, 4 shortform videos + 3-5 edited photos.
- Example: €1,500-€2000 depending on your niche.

**Essential Package**

- Balanced option for most clients.
- Includes more deliverables: e.g. 10 hour shoot, 8 shortform videos (30-60sec), 1 long form (8-12min),10 edited photos, and strategy consultation call.
- Example: €2,500-€3,500.

**Premium Package**

- High-ticket option with maximum value.
- Comprehensive deliverables: e.g., (2) 6 hour shoots, 10 shortform videos (30-60sec), 3 long form (8-12min),15 edited photos, and strategy consultation call.
- Example: €4,000-€6,000+.

<aside>

### **Price Anchoring**

</aside>

Price anchoring is the art of making your mid-tier or premium packages feel like the most logical choice.

- **Position your premium package as aspirational:** Showcase all the value it offers.
- **Make the essential package a no-brainer:** Price it strategically to feel like the best deal for what they get.
- Example: If your Basic is €1,000, your Essential could be €3,000, and your Premium €6,000. This makes the Essential look like the sweet spot.

---

<aside>

### **Retainer Model: Consistent Income**

</aside>

The ultimate goal in your creative business, is to move clients from one-time projects to monthly retainers. Here’s how to pitch it:

- **Highlight consistency:** This is key for clients wanting to drive growth and maintain a consistent digital presence. They will always have consistent, high-quality output for their business.
- **Offer value incentives:** Slightly lower pricing for retainers (e.g., €3,000 per month instead of €4,000 for a one-time shoot).
- **Structure your deliverables:** Be clear about what’s included each month.
- **Start with a 3-6 month contract:** This gives you enough time to show results and locks in consistent revenue.

---

<aside>

### **Payment Terms**

</aside>

Clear payment terms protect both you and your client. Here’s a straightforward system:

- **50% upfront:** Secures your time and starts the project.
- **50% upon delivery:** Final payment is due once the project is complete.
- For retainers: Payments are due on the 1st of each month, ensuring consistent cash flow.

💡 *Pro Tip: Use contracts to clearly outline payment terms and avoid miscommunication.*

---

<aside>

### **Performance-Based Pricing**

</aside>

An innovative and appealing pricing model is **performance-based pricing**, where your earnings are tied to the results your work generates for the client. For example:

- If a client sells a product or service and new customers come through your work, you can negotiate to receive a small percentage of each sale generated from it.
- This approach can be highly attractive to clients because it reduces their upfront risk and shifts the focus to results.

💡 **Hybrid Model:** To ensure you’re covering your costs, you can combine this with a base fee. For example, charge an initial fee to cover production costs and then add a percentage for performance-based results.

Keep in mind:

- **Niche and Industry Suitability:** This model works best for businesses with easily trackable metrics (e.g., e-commerce, online bookings, or service sign-ups).
- **Clear Tracking Systems:** You’ll need to establish a reliable method to track conversions or results from your work. Discuss this with the client beforehand.
- **Flexibility and Negotiation:** Be clear on what the base fee covers and what percentage is fair for both sides.

While this approach can make it easier for clients to say yes, it’s not always suitable for every niche or location. Think critically about whether this aligns with your services and goals.

<aside>

### Additional Tips

</aside>

- **Make sure packages are suitable for your offer**: In some cases depending on your creative service, having just a single option for a specific service / problem might be more effective.
- **Raise your prices as you grow:** As your experience and portfolio expand, gradually increase your rates.
- **Set a minimum project fee:** To avoid small, unprofitable jobs, have a baseline rate (e.g., €1,000).
- **Upsell where possible:** Offer extras like strategy, additional deliverables, or extended support to increase revenue. Covered next in “Value Stacking”.
- **Trial Package:** If you’re just starting but already have some projects under your belt, you can do a trial project at a discounted rate so the client gets a feel for your work.
