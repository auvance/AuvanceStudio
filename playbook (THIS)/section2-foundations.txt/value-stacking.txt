<aside>

Maximizing Client Value & Your Revenue.

</aside>

<aside>

Once you’ve mastered your core offer, the next step is learning how to add complementary services that solve more of your client's problems. This is called **value stacking**—and it's one of the fastest ways to increase your project value without starting from scratch.

</aside>

<aside>

The beauty of value stacking is that you're already working with the client, you understand their brand, and you’re already engaged with their project. Adding complementary services becomes a natural extension of the work you're already doing.

</aside>

---

**Here's how it works:**

Clients don’t want multiple freelancers - they want solutions.

Instead of just delivering a single service, you become a more complete solution for your client. This positions you as more valuable, harder to replace, and justifies higher project fees.

The more problems you solve, the more valuable (and irreplaceable) you become.

### **How to Introduce Value Stacking**

</aside>

**During the Discovery Call:**
Listen for additional pain points beyond your core service. If they mention struggling with social media consistency, website traffic, or lead generation, these are opportunities to expand your offer.

**In Your Proposal:**

Your approach should be tailored to what you discovered during your client conversations. You have two main strategies:

**Strategy 1: Core + Add-Ons**
Present your main offer first, then introduce additional services as optional add-ons. This works well when:

- The client has a specific video need but you've identified other opportunities
- They seem budget-conscious or want to start small
- You're unsure about their full scope of needs

**Strategy 2: Comprehensive Solution**
If your discovery process revealed multiple pain points that you can solve, present a complete package from the start. This works best when:

- The client has expressed frustration with managing multiple vendors
- They have a substantial budget and ambitious goals
- You've identified clear connections between their core service and other services (e.g., they need videos but have no social media strategy to distribute them)

**The key is reading your prospect correctly.** Don't overwhelm someone who just wants a simple brand video with a full marketing package. Conversely, don't undersell to a client who's clearly looking for a comprehensive content solution.

**Pro tip:** When in doubt, present your core offer with a brief mention of additional services: *"This package focuses on our [core service]. Based on our conversation, I also noticed opportunities to help with [specific service]. We can discuss that separately or I can include it in a more comprehensive package if you're interested."*

This gives them the choice while positioning you as someone who sees the bigger picture.

**After Project Completion:**
Use the success of your work to introduce ongoing services.

<aside>

### **Pricing Value-Stacked Offers**

</aside>

When value stacking, don't just add services at cost. The combined package should feel like a deal while still being profitable for you.

**Example:**

- Core video package: €3,000
- Photography add-on: €800 (standalone price: €1,200)
- Social media setup: €500 (standalone price: €800)
- **Total package: €4,300** (vs. €5,000 if purchased separately)

This gives the client a €700 savings while increasing your project value by 43%.

### Expanding Your Capabilities: Learn vs. Outsource

</aside>

You don't need to become an expert in every service you offer. Here are your options:

**Services to Learn Yourself:**

- Skills that are a natural extension of your core service
- Content strategy and planning
- Simple social media management

**Services to Outsource:**

- Advanced SEO and technical website work
- Paid advertising management
- Graphic design and complex brand work
- Copywriting for ads and websites

**Building Your Network:**
Create relationships with freelancers in complementary fields. You can subcontract work while maintaining client relationships and project management.

---

<aside>

### **Implementation Strategy**

</aside>

**Start Small:**
Begin with one additional service that's closest to your existing skills. Choose something closely related to your current service.

**Test and Refine:**
Offer the additional service to 2-3 existing clients at a discounted rate to build case studies and refine your process.

**Create Systems:**
Develop templates, workflows, and pricing structures for each additional service, just like you did for your core service.

**Scale Gradually:**
Add new services only after you've mastered the previous ones. Quality is more important than quantity. Evetually you will have a comprehensive marketing solution that can work for mutliple prospects. 

---

<aside>

### **Value Stacking Red Flags**

</aside>

**Avoid These Mistakes:**

- Adding services you can't deliver well
- Underpricing bundled services
- Overcomplicating your core offer
- Taking on services outside your niche expertise
- Promising results you can't control

**Remember:** Value stacking should enhance your core business, not distract from it. Your core service should remain the hero, with additional offerings supporting and amplifying that core value.

---

The goal isn't to become a jack-of-all-trades, but to become a **comprehensive solution** for your niche. When clients see you as their go-to partner rather than just a service provider or freelancer, you'll command higher fees, enjoy longer client relationships, and build a more sustainable business.
