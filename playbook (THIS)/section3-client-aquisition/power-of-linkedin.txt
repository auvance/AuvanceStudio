How to use Linkedin to close more deals.

</aside>

<aside>

LinkedIn is a goldmine for connecting with potential clients and businesses in your industry. It offers a unique space where professionals engage, network, and collaborate. While platforms like Instagram and TikTok are perfect for building your personal brand and showcasing your creative work, LinkedIn allows you to present a more professional digital presence.

</aside>

---

<aside>

### **Why LinkedIn Matters**

</aside>

Your LinkedIn profile is essentially your online resume and portfolio in one. A strong presence here can help you attract decision-makers and establish credibility in your niche. Here’s what to focus on:

- **Profile Picture:** Use a clean, professional photo that reflects your brand.
- **Headline:** Clearly state what you do and who you help (e.g., “Helping Luxury Hospitality Brands Increase Bookings with High-Quality Media Production”).
- **Experience & Portfolio:** List relevant work experience, highlight key achievements, and link to your website or social media.
- **Engagement:** Share milestones, project success stories, and professional insights to showcase your expertise.

---

<aside>

### **LinkedIn Outreach Strategy**

</aside>

Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of the strategy that’s helped me connect with high-ticket clients:

**1. Identify the Decision-Makers**

When reaching out to a company, look for the individuals responsible for marketing operations. Titles to look for include:

- Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)
- Head of Social Media
- Social Media Manager

If the person isn’t directly responsible, ask them for the contact information of the right individual.

**2. Send an Email First**

Find their email address (using tools like Hunter.io, LinkedIn itself, or the company’s website) and send them your outreach message.

**3. Connect on LinkedIn with a Custom Note**

After sending your email, find the individual on LinkedIn and send a connection request. Include a short, personalized note referencing your email:

📝 **Example:**

"Hi [Name], I just sent you an email about a media collaboration opportunity with [Company]. I’d love to connect here as well!"

(Keep it brief, as LinkedIn has character limits for connection request notes.)

**4. Leverage LinkedIn Premium**

If you have LinkedIn Premium, you can send direct messages without waiting for them to accept your request. This can significantly speed up the process.

---

<aside>

### **Why This Strategy Works**

</aside>

Using multiple points of contact increases the likelihood of your message being seen. Emails can often get lost in spam or overlooked in crowded inboxes. By reaching out on LinkedIn, you’re:

- Reinforcing your initial outreach.
- Demonstrating your persistence and professionalism.
- Making it easier for the client to find and review your email.

This exact strategy helped me land a conversation with the Head of Social Media Marketing for MSC Cruises, leading to a potential collaboration.

---

<aside>

### **Tips for LinkedIn Success**

</aside>

- **Be Consistent:** Engage regularly on LinkedIn by posting updates, sharing valuable insights, and commenting on relevant content.
- **Follow Up:** If you don’t hear back, politely follow up on LinkedIn or Email after a few days.
- **Use Keywords:** Optimize your profile with relevant keywords to make it easier for potential clients to find you.

By leveraging LinkedIn as part of your outreach strategy, you’re not just sending messages—you’re building professional relationships that can lead to long-term collaborations and high-value clients.
