A system to stay in touch with past clients and turn one-time projects into repeat business.

<aside>

Keeping relationships alive with past clients is one of the easiest ways to generate more work. They already know your value, and with the right strategy, you can position yourself as their go-to creative partner.

Aim for retainer models whenever possible. Offering ongoing services creates consistent income and builds long-term partnerships that benefit both you and your clients.

</aside>

---

<aside>

### Quarterly Follow-Ups

</aside>

Every 3 months, reach out via **email or LinkedIn** to check in and offer value:

- Share a quick insight on how they can get more value from what you’ve created
- Remind them of any upcoming events, seasons, or trends that might require new creative work or updates.
- Offer a strategic refresh—suggest ways to repurpose existing work or assets.
- Keep it **casual and helpful**—not just a sales pitch.

> Example follow-up email:
> 
> 
> “Hey [Client’s Name], I hope everything’s going great! I was just looking back at the content we created and wanted to share a quick idea on how you could repurpose / improve it for [upcoming season/trend]. Let me know if you’d like to chat about updating or expanding what we’ve created!”
> 

---

<aside>

### Returning Client Offers & Seasonal Packages

</aside>

Clients love **exclusivity** and **incentives** to come back. Create tailored offers, such as:

- A **returning client discount** on new projects.
- Exclusive **seasonal creative packages** (e.g., summer promotions, holiday campaigns).
- A **subscription model** (e.g., ongoing creative support or refreshes).

> Example: A hotel might need updated content for different seasons, so position yourself as their long-term content partner rather than just a one-time service.
> 

---

<aside>

### Upselling: Repurposing Existing Work

</aside>

Many clients don’t realize that their existing work or assets can be **re-edited and reformatted** to stay fresh:

- Turn past work into new assets or formats.
- Adjust color grading or overlays to match seasonal branding.
- Break down larger deliverables into smaller, usable pieces.
- Add new elements (copy, visuals, or structure) for a fresh angle

Position this as a **cost-effective** way to stay relevant without needing a full new project.

---

<aside>

### Automate & Track Your Follow-Ups

</aside>

Use a **simple CRM, Notion tracker, or reminders** to keep up with past clients:

- Set follow-up reminders **every 3-6 months**.
- Track **which clients** are most likely to book again.
- Note any **seasonal trends** in their business that might require new work or updates.

---

This system ensures that your past clients don’t forget about you and **keeps the door open** for repeat business. When done right, you won’t just be a another contractor—they’ll see you as a **long-term creative partner** who helps their brand grow.
