<aside>

Testimonials are your gateway to your next client. Social proof is essential.

</aside>

<aside>

Collecting testimonials is crucial for building credibility and attracting new clients. However, the process needs to be **quick and effortless** for your clients—aim for a **1-minute response time** to maximize responses.

In the beginning, people will trust other clients more than they trust you. Testimonials do the selling for you.

</aside>

---

<aside>

### How to Collect Testimonials Efficiently

</aside>

You can gather feedback through:

- **Google Forms** (Simple & Free)
- **Typeform** (More Branded Experience)
- **Notion Forms / Tally** (If you want a Notion-integrated workflow)

**Tip:** Keep it **short & clear**—clients are busy, so make it as easy as possible for them to say “yes.”

---

<aside>

### Suggested Testimonial Questions (1-Minute Format)

</aside>

These questions ensure you get valuable insights **without overwhelming** your client:

1. **How would you describe your experience working with us in one sentence?** *(Captures an impactful, short testimonial)*
2. **What did you love most about the final deliverables?** *(Highlights what stood out to them)*
3. **Would you recommend us to others? If yes, why?** *(Encourages referrals & credibility)*
4. **(Optional) Anything we could improve?** *(Shows commitment to growth)*

**Pro Tip:** Keep **Question 1 as required** and make the rest optional to reduce friction.

---

<aside>

### How to Get More Responses

</aside>

- **Send it at the right moment** → Right after delivering the final files, while excitement is high.
- **Make it personal** → A quick message like “We’d love to hear your thoughts! Takes less than a minute 😊” works wonders.
- **Follow up once** → If they haven’t responded in 3-5 days, a friendly reminder helps.

---

<aside>

### What to Do With Testimonials?

</aside>

- Showcase them on your **website, social media, and proposals** to attract more clients.
- **Weave them into your content** – Instead of a loud “Look how great we are!” approach, subtly incorporate them into case studies, captions, highlights, or behind-the-scenes posts.
- **Let your work speak for itself** – Pair testimonials with examples of the work to create a natural connection between the results and the feedback.
- **Use them in conversations** – When pitching to new clients, casually mention what past clients have said about working with you.

The key is to **keep it effortless**—testimonials should feel like an organic part of your brand, not a hard sell.
