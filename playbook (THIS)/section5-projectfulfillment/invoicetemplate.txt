<aside>

Invoices are an essential part of running a professional creative business, ensuring clear communication of payment details and maintaining client trust. While many invoicing platforms like Stripe, QuickBooks, and PayPal streamline the process, a custom-branded invoice template can elevate your client experience.

By sending a clean, professionally branded invoice (which can also be uploaded to the dedicated "Invoices" section in the client portal), you ensure your client feels organized and valued.

A clean billing experience reinforces your positioning just as much as your work does.

</aside>

---

<aside>

### **Why Use a Branded Invoice Template?**

</aside>

**Professionalism:** A branded invoice reinforces your identity as a premium service provider.

**Organization:** Clients can easily keep all documents in one place, simplifying record-keeping.

**Trust:** A well-crafted invoice shows attention to detail, making clients feel secure in their transactions.

---

<aside>

### **Important Notes**

</aside>

**Country-Specific Requirements:**

Invoice requirements can vary depending on your location. Be sure to include all necessary fields, such as:

- Invoice number.
- Date of issuance.
- Client details (name, business name, address).
- Your business details (name, address, tax ID).
- Description of services provided.
- Total amount (with and without taxes, if applicable).
- Payment terms and due dates.
- Governing law (if required).

**Use Online Invoicing Tools When Needed:**

Platforms like Stripe, Wave, and QuickBooks can help you manage payments more easily. However, sending a branded invoice alongside these tools provides an extra touch of professionalism. You can include links to these platforms in your branded invoice.

---

<aside>

### **How to Use the Invoice Template**

</aside>

1. **Customize for Your Brand**
    - Add your business logo, brand colors, and contact information.
2. **Fill Out Service Details**
    - Clearly list the services provided, including project descriptions, dates, and individual costs.
    - Add any relevant taxes or fees and calculate the final total.
3. **Upload to the Client Portal**
    - Save your invoice as a PDF and upload it to the "Documents" section in the client portal for easy access.
4. **Send the Invoice**
    - Notify the client via email that their invoice is ready.

Subject: Your Invoice for [Project Name]
