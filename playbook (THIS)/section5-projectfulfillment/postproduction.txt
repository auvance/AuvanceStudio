<aside>

### **Post-Production Workflow**

</aside>

### **1. Immediate Post-Shoot Tasks**

- **Back Up Footage Immediately**:
    - Create at least 2 backups (external hard drive and cloud storage).
    - Use a reliable folder structure (e.g., *Project Name > RAW Footage > Date/Location*).
- **Verify Files**:
    - Check that all footage and audio files have been successfully transferred.
    - Scrub through files to ensure there are no corrupt or missing clips.

---

<aside>

### **2. Organizing Files**

</aside>

- **Create a Master Folder Structure**:
    - *Project Name*
        - RAW Footage
        - Audio
        - Pre-Production (storyboards, shot lists, etc.)
        - Exports
        - Revisions
        - Final Deliverables

---

<aside>

### **3. Set Up Your Editing Project**

</aside>

- **Software Setup**:
    - Create a project folder in your editing software (Premiere Pro, Final Cut, DaVinci, etc.).
    - Match the project settings to the footage (resolution, frame rate, color space).
- **Import Files**:
    - Organize clips into bins or folders within your software (e.g., by scene, location, or type).
- **Reference Pre-Production Assets**:
    - Storyboard, shot list, and any client notes for reference.

---

<aside>

### **4. Editing Workflow**

</aside>

- **First Pass**:
    - Do a rough cut to establish the flow and structure.
    - Focus on story continuity rather than perfection.
- **Fine-Tuning**:
    - Refine cuts, transitions, and pacing.
    - Sync music and sound effects with visuals.
- **Color Correction and Grading**:
    - Ensure consistent exposure and white balance across clips.
    - Apply creative color grading to match the desired mood.
- **Audio Mixing**:
    - Level out dialogue, music, and sound effects.
    - Use noise reduction or EQ adjustments as needed.

---

<aside>

### **5. Quality Control**

</aside>

- **Review the Edit**:
    - Watch the video from start to finish without distractions.
    - Check for continuity, glitches, and missing elements.
- **Client Revisions**:
    - Create a checklist for feedback to ensure all revisions are addressed.

---

<aside>

### **6. Deliverables**

</aside>

- **Export Settings**:
    - Export in the required resolution, frame rate, and codec (e.g., H.264 for web).
- **Branded Presentation**:
    - Use your client portal for delivery (Google Drive/dropbox link).
    - Include a short message or guide on how to use the files (e.g., best platform for upload).

---

<aside>

### **7. Backup and Archive**

</aside>

- **Final Backup**:
    - Save the final project files, raw footage, and deliverables in a dedicated folder.
- **Cloud Storage**:
    - Use cloud services (e.g., Google Drive, Dropbox) for long-term storage.

---

<aside>

### **Tips for Seamless Workflow**

</aside>

1. **Stay Organized**: A well-organized file structure saves hours during editing.
2. **Save Regularly**: Use autosave features and manual saves frequently.
3. **Refer to Pre-Production Planning**: Stay aligned with the client’s vision by regularly revisiting shot lists, notes, and goals.
4. **Use Templates**: Create templates for export settings, title cards, and branding elements to save time.
