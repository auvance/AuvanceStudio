<aside>

Creating a seamless and professional delivery experience.

</aside>

<aside>

Proper delivery ensures your clients feel valued and have easy access to their final assets, enhancing their overall experience and setting you apart as a premium service provider.

</aside>

---

<aside>

### **1. Organize and Finalize Files**

</aside>

- Export all deliverables in the agreed formats (e.g., video, images, documents, or design files).
- Label files clearly (e.g., “ProjectName_Final_V1” or "HotelName_Instagram_Reel").
- Create a folder structure to organize files (e.g. “Final Deliverables,” “Assets,” “Supporting Files”).

---

<aside>

### **2. Use Feedback Tools**

</aside>

- **Tools like Frame.io, Loom, or collaborative platforms**: Upload drafts for client review. These tools allow for clear, structured feedback on deliverables.
- Encourage clients to consolidate feedback to avoid back-and-forth confusion.

---

<aside>

### **3. Upload to the Client Portal**

</aside>

- **Centralize Everything**: Add the final assets to the **Client Portal** in the "Deliverables" section.

---

<aside>

### **4. Delivery Email or Notification**

</aside>

- Send a **final delivery email** or portal notification to let the client know the assets are ready.
- Include a link to the client portal and a message like:*"Your final deliverables are now available in your client portal. We've also included best practices for using the deliverables. If you need anything else, feel free to reach out!"*

---

<aside>

### **5. Provide Best Practices or Guidelines (Content usage guide)**

</aside>

- Share tips for using the deliverables effectively (e.g., "Post at optimal times for engagement" or "Make sure to check the upload at highest quality option"). This really depends on your project but the idea is to provide as much value and guidelines to the client as possible.

---

<aside>

### **6. Backup Final Assets**

</aside>

- Store copies of all final deliverables on your end for at least 6–12 months in case the client loses access or needs re-delivery.

---

<aside>

### **7. Collect Feedback**

</aside>

- Ask for client feedback on the final deliverables and the overall process. Make sure to do this as soon as you get final approval and the client is satisfied and happy.

---

<aside>

### **8. Continuous Access**

</aside>

- Keep the portal active for an extended period (e.g., 6 months) to allow the client time to access their assets as needed.
- If needed, provide an option for the client to download all files in a zip format for offline storage.

---

<aside>

### **Optional Enhancements**

</aside>

- **Branded Delivery Pages**: Use tools like MediaZilla or Pixieset to create a custom, branded page for the final deliverables.
- **Client Training Videos**: Record a brief walkthrough showing the client how to access and use the deliverables.
